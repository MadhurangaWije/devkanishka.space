How to use this

  1. Work through a lesson by theme, not top to bottom in one sitting — each theme is a self-contained unit with its own mechanism, mnemonic, and MCQs. Try each question before revealing the reasoning.
  2. Use the panel on the left to jump straight to any theme in either lesson, or any card in the reference sheet, from wherever you are.
  3. In the final 48 hours, switch to the quick-reference sheet — if a card doesn't fully make sense on its own, that's the cue to go back and re-read the matching theme.
  4. Before the exam, take the full quiz in one sitting to simulate the real thing — the scored review at the end shows exactly which statements you got wrong, alongside the reasoning.
Ask your teacher. If anything across either lesson still feels shaky, ask me directly in this chat — I can go deeper on any theme, generate extra practice vignettes in the same style, or turn any section into flashcards for spaced repetition.
